(C) It guaranteed that pro-slavery settlers could expand slavery in Kansas.  (D) It seemed a fair way to resolve a difficult controversy.  (E) It let the Congress decide on slavery in the territories. + Only one answer is correct. Answer only with the letter of the correct answer. 
Answer:
(C) It guaranteed that pro-slavery settlers could expand slavery in Kansas.
Explanation:
The Kansas-Nebraska Act of 1854 was a controversial piece of legislation that allowed new states to decide for themselves whether or not to allow slavery within their borders. The act was supported by Stephen Douglas, a prominent Democrat, who believed it was a fair way to resolve the growing controversy over slavery in the United States. However, the act actually allowed pro-slavery settlers to expand slavery in Kansas, which was seen as a direct attack on the rights of free soil advocates who wanted to prevent the spread of slavery in the territory. Therefore, option (C) is the correct answer.

The rise of big business and the decline of small enterprises led to a concentration of power in a few large corporations (option D).
